Description

This recipe was passed down to me from my uncle's uncle who himself stole it from the cookbook of his third wife's aunt, who before whe died had cursed the recipe to be so delicious that only the sturdiest of ovens could reach the hell and brimstone level temperatures that are required to bake the cheese properly. this was before the advent of electric fan ovens so rest assured that you are safe and well to continue.

Ingredients

  • Anything about to go off in the fridge
  • hot water
  • Salt. A lot of salt
  • blender
  • tuppaware
  1. grab food from fridge that looks about half a day from being in the bin.
  2. Chop it up roughly into chunks, remving any bits with mould or that smell funny.
  3. bang it all in a pan over a medium heat with a fair knob of butter and olive oil.
  4. boil enough water to cover in the kettle and keep the veg moving until the kettle boils.
  5. pour in water to pan and season to your liking. Add any old herbs you;ve got knicking about, whats the worst that can happen? it was all bound for the bin anyway.
  6. boil and then simmer for about 10/15 mins until the veg is soft.
  7. bang it all in a blender and add a splash of either milk or cream if it looks a bit thin.
  8. blend but leave a few chunks in it. fibres good for you.
  9. serve with the last remaining chunks of bread from the bread bin. toast if stale.
  10. Bon appetit
